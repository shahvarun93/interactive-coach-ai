Messaging Queue Trade-offs

Why Use Queues?

  • Decouple producers and consumers.
  • Smooth spikes (buffer work), implement backpressure.
  • Retry and dead-letter handling for failures.

Delivery Semantics

  • At-most-once: no duplicates, but messages may be lost.
  • At-least-once: no loss, but duplicates possible (most common).
  • Exactly-once: often simulated via idempotent consumers.

Ordering & Partitioning

  • Per-partition ordering vs global ordering trade-offs.
  • Keyed partitioning (e.g., by user_id) to maintain per-entity order.

Interview Checklist

  • Explain why a queue is needed (what problem it solves).
  • Describe delivery semantics and how consumers handle duplicates.
  • Cover scaling (more partitions / consumers), and backpressure.
  • Mention dead-letter queues and monitoring.
